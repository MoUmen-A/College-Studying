Consider the typical operation of automatic washing machine. When the machine is simply plugged in but not running, it's ready for use. A user will load clothes, add detergent, close the door, and then select a wash program ("Cotton," "Delicate"). Once the "Start" button is pressed, the machine first fills with water. After the water level is sufficient, it begins the main washing agitation cycle. Following this, the dirty water is drained, and the machine proceeds to fill with clean water for the rinsing phase. After rinsing, the water is drained again, and a high-speed spin cycle begins to remove excess water from the clothes. Once the spin cycle is complete, the machine indicates that the wash is finished, and the door can be safely opened. At any point before the "Wash Complete" indication, if the user presses "Pause" or opens the door, the current cycle will halt, and the machine will wait for input.

Question 4 - Case Study- Comprehensive UML System Specification (15 Marks)
Hospital Appointment Scheduling System (HASS)
The Hospital Appointment Scheduling System (HASS) is a proposed web-based and mobile-accessible application aimed at streamlining the process of booking and managing patient appointments within a hospital or clinic network. Traditionally, patients would call or visit the hospital, leading to long hold times, miscommunications, and inefficient use of staff time. The HASS seeks to enhance patient convenience, optimize resource allocation, and reduce administrative overhead.

The system allows patients to register for a new account or log in using their existing credentials. Once logged in, a patient can search for available appointments by specifying a doctor's name, a medical specialty (such as Cardiology, Dermatology, etc.), or a preferred date/time. The system displays available slots, factoring in doctor's availability and clinic hours. After selecting a suitable slot, the patient can book the appointment, receiving an immediate confirmation via email or SMS. Patients also have the ability to view their upcoming appointments, reschedule existing appointments to a different time or date, or cancel appointments if necessary. The system sends automated reminders to patients before their scheduled visit.

Medical staff, including receptionists and nurses, can also log into the system. They primarily use the system to manage the daily schedule for their assigned doctors or departments. They can view a doctor's full calendar, book appointments on behalf of patients, and mark appointments as 'checked-in' upon patient arrival. They can also update patient details or record basic visit notes (though detailed electronic health records are handled by a separate, integrated system).

Doctors have read-only access to their personal appointment schedules. They can view their daily or weekly patient list, including patient details relevant to the appointment, and see the status of each appointment.

===== Page 4 =====

Hospital administrators possess higher-level access. They can manage doctor profiles (adding new doctors, updating specialties, managing leave), configure available clinic services and departments, and set up operating hours for various clinics. Administrators can also generate comprehensive reports, such as daily appointment summaries, monthly patient visit statistics, or doctor workload analysis, to aid in resource planning and operational efficiency. The HASS is designed to ensure all appointment data is accurate, secure, and readily accessible to authorized personnel, significantly improving the overall patient experience and hospital management.
