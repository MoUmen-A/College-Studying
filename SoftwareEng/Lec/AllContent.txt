1) Core idea: what is a software process?-Software process: a structured set of activities needed to develop a software system.-All software processes include four main activities: Specification, Design and implementation, Validation,
and Evolution.-Software process model: an abstract representation of a software process from a particular perspective.-When describing a process, mention activities, their order, products, roles, and pre/post-conditions.
Term Simple meaning
Product Outcome of a process activity, such as a requirements document or test report.
Role Responsibility of a person in the process, such as developer, tester, manager, customer.
Pre-condition Something true before the activity starts.
Post-condition Something true after the activity finishes.
2) Plan-driven vs agile
Point Plan-driven process Agile process
Planning Activities are planned in advance. Planning is incremental and flexible.
Progress measure Measured against the original plan. Measured through working increments and
feedback.
Change Harder to change after the plan starts. Designed to accept changing requirements.
Reality Useful when requirements are stable. Useful when requirements are unclear or
changing.
Exam sentence Plan first, follow plan. Build in small steps and adapt.
Memorize
There is no universally right or wrong software process. In practice, most real projects mix plan-driven and
agile elements.

4) Waterfall model
Lecture diagram: Incremental development interleaves
specification, development, validation.- Waterfall is a plan-driven model with separate and distinct phases.- Phases: requirements analysis and definition -> system/software design -> implementation and unit
testing -> integration and system testing -> operation and maintenance.- Main drawback: difficult to accommodate change after the process is underway.- Appropriate only when requirements are well understood and changes are limited.- Mostly used in large systems engineering projects where many sites must coordinate work.
Essay answer skeleton: Waterfall
Waterfall is a sequential plan-driven model. It is simple and organized because each phase has defined
outputs, but it is inflexible because changing requirements after starting the project causes expensive
rework. Therefore, it is best for stable requirements and large coordinated engineering projects.

1) Embedded software and responsiveness- Embedded software controls systems from domestic machines to manufacturing plants.- It reacts to events from hardware and may issue control signals in response.- It is usually embedded in hardware, often in read-only memory, and responds in real time.- Responsiveness is the critical difference between embedded systems and ordinary software.
Core definition
A real-time system is a software system where correct functioning depends on the results produced and
the time at which those results are produced.
Type
Soft real-time system
Meaning
Operation is degraded if results are not produced according to timing
requirements.
Hard real-time system
Operation is incorrect if results are not produced according to timing specification.
2) Characteristics of embedded systems- They usually run continuously and do not terminate.- Interactions with the environment are unpredictable.- Physical limitations may affect design.- Direct hardware interaction may be necessary.- Safety and reliability may dominate the design.
3) Embedded system design- Design is a systems engineering process that must consider hardware design and performance.- Designers decide which capabilities are implemented in software and which in hardware.- Low-level hardware, support software, and timing decisions must be considered early.- Extra functionality such as battery and power management may be required.
